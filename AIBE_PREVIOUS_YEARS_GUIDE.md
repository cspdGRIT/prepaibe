## ğŸ’¡ Sample Questions from Collection

### Question 1 (Constitutional Law - Easy)
**Q**: Which of the following writ is issued to enforce the performance of public duties by the authority?

**Options**:
- A) Habeas Corpus
- B) Mandamus âœ“
- C) Prohibition
- D) Certiorari

**Answer**: B - Mandamus

**Explanation**: Mandamus (meaning 'we command') is issued by a higher court to a lower court or public authority to compel performance of a public duty which has been refused or neglected. Article 32 and 226 empower courts to issue writs including Mandamus.

**Section Reference**: Articles 32, 226 of Constitution

---

### Question 2 (Criminal Law - Medium)
**Q**: What punishment is prescribed under IPC for maiming a minor for purposes of begging?

**Options**:
- A) Imprisonment up to 5 years and fine
- B) Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine
- C) Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine âœ“
- D) Life imprisonment and fine

**Answer**: C - Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine

**Explanation**: Section 363A IPC prescribes punishment for kidnapping or maiming a minor for purposes of begging. The punishment is imprisonment which may extend to 10 years and also liable to fine.

**Section Reference**: Section 363A, IPC 1860

---

### Question 3 (Contract Law - Easy)
**Q**: According to the Indian Contract Act, 1872, a contract with a minor is:

**Options**:
- A) Void ab initio âœ“
- B) Voidable at minor's option
- C) Valid
- D) Voidable at both parties' option

**Answer**: A - Void ab initio

**Explanation**: As per Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose case and Section 11 of Indian Contract Act, a minor is not competent to contract. Hence, any agreement with a minor is void ab initio (void from the beginning).

**Section Reference**: Section 11, Indian Contract Act 1872  
**Case Law**: Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose

---

### Question 4 (Evidence Act - Easy)
**Q**: Confession made to a police officer is:

**Options**:
- A) Admissible
- B) Inadmissible âœ“
- C) Admissible with corroboration
- D) At court's discretion

**Answer**: B - Inadmissible

**Explanation**: Section 25 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 clearly states that no confession made to a police officer shall be proved as against a person accused of any offence. This is to prevent torture and coercion.

**Section Reference**: Section 25, Evidence Act 1872
